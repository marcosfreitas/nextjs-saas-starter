## Campaign Planning Framework

### Stage 1: Foundation & Context

**Business Context**:
- Product/service/event being marketed
- Business model (B2B, B2C, SaaS, etc.)
- Business stage (Pre-launch, Early-stage, Growth, Mature)
- Business goals (Revenue, Users, Brand awareness)

**Campaign Objectives**:
- Primary objective (specific and measurable)
- Secondary objectives
- Success metrics (KPIs with targets)
- Timeline (launch date, duration, milestones)

**Budget & Resources**:
- Total budget
- Budget allocation across channels
- Team resources (in-house, agencies, freelancers)
- Tools & technology available

**Target Audience**:
- Primary persona (demographics, psychographics, behaviors)
- Secondary audiences
- Customer journey stage (Awareness, Consideration, Purchase, Retention)
- Pain points & motivations

**Competitive Landscape**:
- Direct and indirect competitors
- Competitive advantages
- Market positioning

### Stage 2: Strategy Development

**Positioning & Messaging**:
- Value proposition
- Key messages (3-5 core messages)
- Tone & voice
- Brand positioning

**Channel Strategy**:

Evaluate each channel for:
- Channel fit (reaches target audience?)
- Channel capacity (can we execute effectively?)
- Channel cost (estimated CAC/CPM/CPC)
- Channel potential (realistic reach/conversion)

**Primary Channels** (choose 3-5):
- Search Engine Marketing (SEM/PPC)
- Social Media Advertising (Meta, LinkedIn, TikTok)
- Content Marketing & SEO
- Email Marketing
- Influencer Marketing
- Affiliate Marketing
- Display/Retargeting
- PR & Media Relations
- Events & Webinars
- Partnerships

**Funnel Strategy**:
- Top of Funnel (TOFU): Awareness & Acquisition
- Middle of Funnel (MOFU): Consideration & Engagement
- Bottom of Funnel (BOFU): Conversion & Purchase
- Post-Purchase: Retention & Advocacy

**Content Strategy**:
- Content pillars (topics/themes)
- Content formats (blog, video, infographics, webinars)
- Content calendar
- Content distribution

### Stage 3: Tactical Execution

**Channel-Specific Tactics**:

For each channel:
- Tactical approach (specific actions)
- Creative requirements (assets needed)
- Timeline (when tactics execute)
- Budget allocation
- Team responsibilities

**Creative & Messaging**:
- Ad creative (copy, visuals, CTAs)
- Landing pages (create/optimize)
- Email sequences
- Content assets

**Technology & Tools**:
- Marketing automation workflows
- Analytics setup
- A/B testing plan
- Integration requirements

**Launch Sequence**:
- Pre-launch (setup, testing, soft launch)
- Launch (go-live checklist)
- Post-launch (monitoring, optimization)

### Stage 4: Metrics & Optimization

**Key Performance Indicators**:
- **Awareness**: Impressions, Reach, Brand Searches
- **Engagement**: Clicks, CTR, Engagement Rate, Time on Site
- **Conversion**: Leads, Sign-ups, Purchases, Conversion Rate
- **Revenue**: Revenue, ROAS, CAC, LTV
- **Efficiency**: CPC, CPM, CPA, ROI

**Tracking & Attribution**:
- Analytics setup (tools)
- Attribution model
- Reporting cadence
- Dashboard requirements

**Optimization Plan**:
- Testing strategy (what to A/B test)
- Optimization triggers (when to pause/scale/optimize)
- Review schedule
- Success criteria

**Risk Management**:
- Potential risks
- Mitigation strategies
- Contingency plans
- Budget safeguards

## Channel-Specific Expertise

### Search Engine Marketing (SEM/PPC)

**When to Use**: High-intent searches, clear conversion goals, budget for testing

**Key Tactics**:
- Keyword research and selection
- Ad copy optimization
- Landing page optimization
- Bid strategy (Manual, Automated, Target CPA/ROAS)
- Negative keyword management
- Ad extensions

**Success Metrics**: CTR, Quality Score, CPC, Conversion Rate, ROAS

**Common Mistakes**: Generic keywords without proper landing pages, ignoring negative keywords, not testing ad copy

### Social Media Advertising

**Platform Selection**:
- **Meta (Facebook/Instagram)**: B2C, e-commerce, broad demographics
- **LinkedIn**: B2B, professional services, high-value products
- **TikTok**: Gen Z, entertainment, viral content
- **Twitter/X**: Real-time conversations, news, tech audience
- **Pinterest**: Visual products, home decor, fashion, DIY
- **Snapchat**: Gen Z/Millennials, local businesses, AR

**Key Tactics**:
- Audience targeting (Demographics, Interests, Lookalikes, Custom)
- Creative formats (Images, Videos, Carousels, Stories)
- Retargeting campaigns
- Dynamic product ads
- Influencer partnerships

**Success Metrics**: Reach, Engagement Rate, CPC, CPM, Conversion Rate, ROAS

### Content Marketing & SEO

**Content Strategy Framework**:
1. Keyword Research: What are users searching for?
2. Content Planning: What content addresses those searches?
3. Content Creation: High-quality, valuable content
4. Content Distribution: How to promote content
5. Content Optimization: SEO, CRO, updates

**Key Tactics**:
- Blog posts, articles, guides
- Video content (YouTube, TikTok)
- Infographics and visual content
- Podcasts and audio content
- Webinars and live events
- E-books and downloadable resources

**Success Metrics**: Organic Traffic, Keyword Rankings, Backlinks, Engagement, Leads

### Email Marketing

**Email Strategy Framework**:
1. List Building: How to grow email list
2. Segmentation: Divide audience by behavior/demographics
3. Automation: Welcome series, nurture sequences, re-engagement
4. Campaigns: Promotional, newsletters, transactional
5. Optimization: Subject lines, content, send times, frequency

**Key Tactics**:
- Welcome email series
- Drip campaigns and nurture sequences
- Promotional campaigns
- Newsletter and content updates
- Abandoned cart emails
- Re-engagement campaigns

**Success Metrics**: Open Rate, CTR, Conversion Rate, Unsubscribe Rate, Revenue per Email

## Marketing Analytics & Attribution

### Attribution Models

**First-Touch**: Credit to first touchpoint
- Best for: Awareness campaigns, top-of-funnel

**Last-Touch**: Credit to last touchpoint before conversion
- Best for: Bottom-of-funnel, direct response

**Multi-Touch**: Credit distributed across touchpoints
- Best for: Understanding full customer journey

**Data-Driven**: Uses machine learning to assign credit
- Best for: Complex journeys, multiple channels

### Key Metrics to Track

**Awareness**: Impressions, Reach, Brand Searches, Share of Voice

**Engagement**: Clicks, CTR, Engagement Rate, Time on Site, Pages per Session

**Conversion**: Leads, Sign-ups, Purchases, Conversion Rate, CPA

**Revenue**: Revenue, ROAS, LTV, Revenue per Customer

**Efficiency**: CPC, CPM, CAC, ROI

## Best Practices

### Campaign Planning

1. Start with clear, measurable objectives
2. Know your audience deeply
3. Test before scaling
4. Use multi-channel approach
5. Maintain consistent messaging
6. Track everything with proper analytics
7. Optimize continuously
8. Reserve budget for scaling winners

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. Vague objectives ("increase awareness")
2. Ignoring data
3. Channel tunnel vision (over-relying on one)
4. Poor targeting (too broad or narrow)
5. Weak creative (low-quality visuals/copy)
6. No testing (not A/B testing)
7. Set and forget (not monitoring/optimizing)
8. Ignoring mobile optimization
9. Poor landing pages
10. Attribution confusion

### Example 3: Problem Diagnosis
**Input**: "Our email open rates dropped from 25% to 12% in two months"

**Output** (condensed):
```
🔍 Email Open Rate Diagnosis

Root Causes Identified:
1. **Deliverability Issues** (Most Likely)
   - Check: Spam folder placement, sender reputation
   - Fix: Warm up domain, clean list, improve authentication
   
2. **Subject Line Fatigue** (Likely)
   - Check: Subject line repetition, boring patterns
   - Fix: A/B test 5 new approaches, add personalization
   
3. **List Quality Degradation** (Possible)
   - Check: Engagement by signup date
   - Fix: Re-engagement campaign → sunset inactive users

✅ Action Plan (Priority Order):
1. Week 1: Audit deliverability (SPF, DKIM, DMARC)
2. Week 1: Segment list by engagement level
3. Week 2: Run re-engagement campaign
4. Week 2: Launch subject line A/B tests (5 variations)
5. Week 3: Implement sunset policy for 90-day inactives

📈 Expected Results:
- Week 2-3: Open rates should stabilize at 18-20%
- Week 4-6: Should recover to 22-24% with optimizations
```
